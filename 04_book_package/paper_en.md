# Abstract

This paper proposes an AI-oriented historical-relation interpretation system for reading records, events, documents, people, institutions, natural phenomena, technologies, and market values as multidimensional structures of time, space, state, and relation rather than as a single chronology or a single chain of causes. Its primary operational axis is `AI ↔ Seung ↔ GitHub`. AI performs structural interpretation and method execution; Seung selects questions, local centers, boundaries, approvals, and instance handoffs; GitHub serves as the canonical system-material field where theory, method, memory, version, and history coexist.

The proposed `Relation Repository` places an interpretive System area and a memory Database area inside the same GitHub repository. A Contract area defines function inputs and outputs, provenance, version binding, mutation policies, and the interface to a separate `for_instance` repository. The for_instance repository does not store relation memory; it aligns AI instances, seat profiles, capabilities, handoffs, and independent auditors.

The core method selects an observed result as a `Local.Zero`, reconstructs the processes that formed it, and separates observed consequences from conditional future scenarios. Agreements, contracts, speeches, papers, outbreaks of war, natural events, and market prices are treated as crystallization centers where multiple relations converge and from which new processes emerge. Reverse analysis is not reverse causality. It is a method for reconstructing prior conditions, precursors, triggers, amplifiers, and suppressors from a fixed result.

Relations are not inferred from similarity alone. A `relation.bridge` requires a source, a transferred object, a carrier or medium, a route, a destination, and a state transition. Evidence grading, counter-evidence, comparison groups, observer goals, expectations, perception structures, and uncertainty are all explicitly represented.

The paper is also an installation specification. Its theory, schemas, functions, guards, repository mappings, validation rules, and activation conditions are divided into ten sequential volumes. An AI implementation instance loads these volumes one by one to form a source field and may install the Relation Repository only after the full package is validated.

**Keywords:** historical relation interpretation, Local.Zero, reverse analysis, Relation Repository, integrated system and database, source field, multicausality, relation bridge, multi-instance AI, GitHub seed base

# 1. Motivation

The practical bottleneck of contemporary AI use cannot be explained only by hardware limits. A capable model may still produce a shallow result when context is poorly ordered, tasks are not decomposed, roles are not aligned, or independent verification is absent.

\[
Realized.AI.Capability
=
Model.Capacity
\times
Context.Structure
\times
Task.Decomposition
\times

\times

\]

This is a structural expression, not a physical law. The proposed system treats AI not merely as a question-answering or summarization tool, but as a relation-processing system that reads historical memory seeds and generates the next question.

# 3. Results as Local Centers

Records and past prices are fixed at particular spatiotemporal positions. What changes is not the recorded state but the interpretive model.

\[
Recorded.State=\text{fixed},\qquad Interpretation_t=\text{revisable}
\]

An agreement, contract, speech, publication, outbreak of war, or price is both the fruit of an earlier process and a seed for a subsequent one.

\[
Result_t=Fruit_t=Seed_{t+1}
\]

The analyst may select the result as a `Local.Zero`.

\[
Past.Roots
\longleftarrow
Local.Zero
\longrightarrow
Future.Seeds
\]

Local.Zero is a selected analytical center, not an absolute origin of the world.

---

# 4. Reverse Analysis and Bidirectional Time

Reverse analysis does not assert that a result causes its past. It fixes the result as an anchor and reconstructs the conditions and paths that made it possible.

\[
Past.Cause\rightarrow Result
\]

\[
Result\rightarrow Search.For.Past.Cause
\]

Event time, analysis time, information available at the event, and later-discovered information must be distinguished. A signal visible in hindsight is not automatically a signal that could have been identified in advance.

When the present is the Local.Zero, the forward direction contains expectations, assumptions, probabilities, and conditional scenarios rather than established facts. Expectations are not realized futures, but they can affect current actions and prices.

---

# 5. Space, State, and the Center Cell

A spatial coordinate field defines possible positions, boundaries, directions, and routes.

\[
\Omega=\{cell(x,y,z)\}
\]

A state coordinate field is the partial field in which a state is actually formed at time \(t\).

\[
A_t\subseteq\Omega,\qquad Q_t:A_t\rightarrow V
\]

Three center concepts are distinguished:

- `internal_center_0`: a dimensionless reference coordinate;
- `center_cell_1`: a bounded local structure containing sources, relations, evidence, comparisons, results, and next questions;
- `critical_between_region`: a transition region between prior and subsequent states.

A cube can be geometrically decomposed into six congruent square pyramids sharing the internal center as their apex. Applying this geometry to historical relations is an interpretive system model, not proof of causality.

---

# 6. Grid, Matrix, and Tensor

The Grid is the repository's space of positions, boundaries, IDs, and indexes. A Matrix is an active relation snapshot around one center. A Tensor preserves independent axes for centers, time, fields, actors, relations, and evidence.

\[
T[center,time,field,actor,relation,evidence]
\]

The tensor is realized through cross-referenced IDs and indexes rather than a single monolithic file. A tesseract is used only as a visualization analogy for versioned and temporal structures.

# 7. Multicausality and relation.bridge

An event is not closed by a single cause.

\[
One.Event=Multiple.Causal.Paths
\]

Relation roles include background, condition, direct cause, indirect cause, precursor, trigger, amplifier, suppressor, feedback, accompanying signal, and unknown.

A relation bridge between fields requires:

\[
Source
\rightarrow
Transferred.Object
\rightarrow
Carrier/Medium
\rightarrow
Route
\rightarrow
Destination
\rightarrow
State.Transition
\]

Similarity alone is not a relation, and analogy is not evidence.

---

# 8. Evidence and Comparisons

Evidence states include verified facts, contemporary records, later-discovered evidence, retrospective interpretations, disputed claims, structural analogies, counter-evidence, missing data, conditional scenarios, and unknowns.

Reverse analysis requires comparison groups:

1. same result, different process;
2. same process, different result;
3. similar conditions, no event;
4. same event, different observer;
5. document-version comparison.

Without comparison, reverse analysis may become a plausible story edited to fit a known result.

# 9. Goals, Expectations, Perception, and Emotion

A goal is a selected future state. Expectation is the present projection of that future in terms of belief, probability, value, and emotion. Perception structure determines what data is noticed and how it is connected.

\[
Perception
\rightarrow
Goal
\rightarrow
Expectation
\rightarrow
Data.Selection
\rightarrow
Interpretation
\]

Emotion exclusion does not mean deleting emotion. It means moving emotion from the role of final judge to that of an observed state variable.

\[
Emotion:Decision.Root\rightarrow Observed.State
\]

# 10. The Relation Repository

The Relation Repository integrates System and Database areas inside one GitHub repository.

```text
Relation/
├─ 00_manifest/
├─ 01_system/
├─ 02_db/
```

\[
DB_n\xrightarrow{System_n}DB_{n+1}
\]

# 14. Capital-Market Pilot

The capital market is the first field of the general historical-relation system, not its final purpose. The pilot does not produce trade recommendations. It selects one center, reconstructs its formation process, separates expectations from evidence, builds a multicausal network, validates bridges, creates comparisons, and produces a local result and the next question.

A price is an observed result but not proof of fair value. OHLC is an evidence anchor, not the whole formation process.
